Check iceberg lettuce before you eat it

A July 2026 recall covers iceberg lettuce and shredded lettuce linked to an ongoing Cyclospora outbreak. Check the brand, package size, and date before eating—and discard recalled products rather than trying to wash them safe.

Check your refrigerator: Look for Marketside Iceberg Salad in 12- or 24-ounce bags and Marketside Shredded Lettuce in 8- or 16-ounce bags with dates from July 18 through August 3, 2026. The FDA notice also lists affected food-service products.
Do not eat recalled lettuce: Throw it away or return it for a refund. Do not taste it to decide whether it is safe.
Clean contact surfaces: Wash and sanitize refrigerator shelves, containers, cutting boards, utensils, and counters that touched the recalled lettuce.
Ask when eating out: When the source is unknown, ask whether the restaurant’s iceberg lettuce is part of the recall.
Watch for symptoms: Frequent diarrhea, stomach cramps, bloating, nausea, fatigue, or symptoms that improve and return can occur. Contact a health-care provider, especially after eating shredded iceberg lettuce in the prior two weeks.
Why washing is not enough: CDC says routine chemical disinfection or sanitization is unlikely to kill Cyclospora. Standard produce washing is still good practice for non-recalled produce, but a recalled product should be discarded.
The FDA investigation was still ongoing on July 19, 2026. Product distribution and affected locations may expand, so recheck the official outbreak page before using iceberg lettuce.
