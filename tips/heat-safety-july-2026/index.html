Beat dangerous heat before it beats you

When a Heat Advisory is active, postpone strenuous outdoor activity during the hottest part of the day. Hydrate, use air conditioning or a cooling space, and check on older adults, children, neighbors, and anyone with health conditions.

Move activity: Schedule exercise and outdoor work for early morning or evening.
Cool down: Stay in air conditioning when possible and take frequent shaded or cooled breaks.
Hydrate: Drink water regularly rather than waiting until you feel thirsty.
Check others: Contact relatives and neighbors who may be more vulnerable to heat.
Protect vehicles: Never leave children or pets in an unattended vehicle.
Heat stroke is an emergency. Move the person to a cooler place and call 911 immediately.
